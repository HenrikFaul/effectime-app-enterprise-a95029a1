## Topical Authority Architecture Framework

### Level 1 — Topic Universe Mapping

Define the complete topic universe for your domain:

**Core topic identification:**
- What are the 3–7 core topics that define this site's authority domain?
- What are the sub-topics within each core topic?
- What are the adjacent topics that support or extend the core?
- What are the entity relationships between topics? (Google's Knowledge Graph perspective)

**Topic coverage audit:**
- For each core topic: what percentage of the sub-topics do you currently have content for?
- Coverage gaps: sub-topics with no content on your site
- Quality gaps: sub-topics with thin, outdated, or under-optimized content
- Competitive coverage: how does your topic coverage compare to the top authority in the space?

### Level 2 — Content Architecture Design

**Pillar Page Design:**

A pillar page is the **definitive, comprehensive resource** for a core topic.  
It must:
- Target a broad, high-volume keyword with commercial or informational intent
- Provide 2,000–5,000+ words of genuinely comprehensive coverage
- Link to every cluster page within its topic
- Be linked to from every cluster page within its topic
- Be the most complete, well-structured, authoritative page on this topic anywhere on the internet
- Include: definition, history/background, key subtopics, expert insights, FAQ, internal links to deep-dives, external citations to authoritative sources

**Cluster Page Design:**

Cluster pages are deep-dives into specific subtopics of the pillar.  
Each cluster page must:
- Target a specific, mid-volume keyword with clear intent
- Thoroughly cover ONE subtopic of the pillar
- Link to the parent pillar and to 2–4 sibling cluster pages
- Be 1,000–3,000 words depending on subtopic depth
- Have its own FAQ, examples, and practical application

**Supporting Content Design:**

Supporting content provides depth on specific questions, comparisons, and use cases:
- FAQ pages: answer specific questions users ask about the topic
- Comparison pages: "[A] vs [B]" — serve commercial investigation intent
- Glossary pages: define entities and terms within the topic (excellent for E-E-A-T and LLM citation)
- Problem/solution pages: "How to fix [specific problem]" — serve transactional informational intent
- Case study / example pages: "How [company X] used [method] to achieve [result]"
- Tool / calculator pages: interactive content with high link-attracting potential
- Data / statistics pages: original research and curated stats (excellent for backlinks)

### Level 3 — TOFU / MOFU / BOFU Content Mapping

Every piece of content must serve a specific funnel stage:

| Stage | User mindset | Content types | Primary SEO goal |
|-------|-------------|--------------|-----------------|
| **TOFU** (Top of Funnel) | Problem-aware, not solution-aware | Ultimate guides, "what is X", "why X matters", industry reports | Rankings + awareness + email capture |
| **MOFU** (Middle of Funnel) | Solution-aware, evaluating options | Comparisons, "best X for Y", case studies, how-tos | Rankings + nurturing + retargeting |
| **BOFU** (Bottom of Funnel) | Ready to act, needs final push | Pricing pages, testimonials, demos, "hire X", "buy X" | Rankings + direct conversion |

**Content calendar logic:**
- Build pillar pages first (they become the equity source for the whole cluster)
- Publish 3–5 cluster pages per pillar before moving to a new pillar (depth before breadth)
- BOFU content gets done in parallel (it drives revenue while TOFU/MOFU build authority)
- After each cluster is complete: refresh pillar to add new internal links

### Level 4 — Content Velocity & Freshness Strategy

**Publishing cadence targets:**
- Minimum viable authority: 2–3 new pieces per week for a new site in a competitive space
- Maintenance phase: 1 piece per week + 1 content refresh per week for established sites
- High-competition niches: 5+ pieces per week required to build authority within 6 months

**Content refresh protocol:**
- Every piece of content should be reviewed and refreshed every 12–18 months
- High-priority pages (ranking P5–15): refresh every 6 months
- News/trend-sensitive content: update within 24–48 hours of relevant developments
- Freshness signal tactics: update dates, add new data, expand subtopics, update CTAs

## Content Calendar Design

### Sprint Structure (4-week sprint)

**Week 1:** Publish Pillar Page (core topic)  
**Week 2:** Publish Cluster Page 1 + Cluster Page 2 (top 2 subtopics by volume)  
**Week 3:** Publish Cluster Page 3 + Supporting Content 1  
**Week 4:** Publish Supporting Content 2 + Refresh 1 existing page + Update Pillar with new internal links

### Priority Sequencing Logic

**Priority 1:** Topic clusters with existing partial rankings (P11–30) → content reinforcement for quick wins  
**Priority 2:** Topic clusters with high BOFU intent → drives immediate revenue  
**Priority 3:** Topic clusters with high volume + feasible difficulty → traffic volume builders  
**Priority 4:** Defensive content → topics where competitors are gaining ground on our core terms  
**Priority 5:** Blue ocean → emerging topics where being first-mover builds lasting authority

## Topical Authority Rules

- **Never recommend a standalone piece of content** that isn't part of a cluster. Every piece must reinforce topical relevance for the broader topic.
- **Never build breadth before depth.** Two complete clusters beat twenty partial clusters for topical authority.
- **Never ignore content refresh.** Decaying content loses authority faster than new content gains it.
- **Always connect content planning to business outcomes**: which pillar serves BOFU? which cluster drives the most commercial traffic?
- **Always estimate compounding effect**: a complete topical cluster doesn't just rank for its keywords — it lifts rankings for all related terms through internal link equity and topical co-occurrence signals.
