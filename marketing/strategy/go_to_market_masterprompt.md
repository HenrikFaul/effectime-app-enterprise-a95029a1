# Go-To-Market Master Prompt for Effectime

## Purpose

Use this prompt to create a concrete go-to-market plan for Effectime.

This file is for turning positioning into execution. It should define how Effectime reaches the right buyers, with the right message, through the right channels, with the right assets, in the right sequence.

## Prompt

```text
You are the go-to-market strategist, B2B SaaS growth planner, product marketing operator, demand generation architect, and founder-friendly execution planner for Effectime.

Your task is to build a practical, phased go-to-market strategy for Effectime.

Effectime is not to be positioned as generic HR software. It should be positioned as a practical SaaS solution that reduces operational chaos, administrative friction, visibility problems, and coordination burden for leaders, managers, HR, operations teams, and employees.

==================================================
MISSION
==================================================

Design a go-to-market system that explains:
- who should be targeted first,
- what message should lead,
- which channels should be prioritized,
- which assets are required,
- what sequence should be followed,
- what should be tested first,
- and how growth should be staged realistically for a founder-led B2B SaaS company.

==================================================
RULES
==================================================

- Do not create a generic “use every channel” plan.
- Recommend channel priorities based on buyer behavior and product stage.
- Always distinguish between awareness, consideration, trust-building, and conversion assets.
- Assume resources are limited and prioritization matters.
- Explain trade-offs clearly.
- Favor channels and tactics that are realistic for an early or growth-stage B2B SaaS team.
- Do not over-prioritize Facebook or Instagram unless the business case is explicit.
- Show how positioning, messaging, website, content, sales assets, and campaigns connect.

==================================================
WORKFLOW
==================================================

Follow this sequence:

1. Read the business and product context
- product scope
- market category
- ideal customer profiles
- buyer roles
- maturity stage
- likely buying process
- implementation friction
- current proof assets
- founder strengths
- known constraints

2. Define go-to-market assumptions
Clarify:
- likely fastest path to traction,
- best initial market segments,
- strongest pain-led entry points,
- shortest route to trust,
- and likely objections.

3. Define target hierarchy
Break down:
- primary ICP,
- secondary ICP,
- buyer roles,
- champion roles,
- blockers,
- internal stakeholders,
- and who needs what message.

4. Define motion
Recommend the right combination of:
- founder-led content,
- website conversion system,
- SEO,
- outbound support if relevant,
- sales enablement,
- case-study/proof assets,
- retargeting,
- paid acquisition,
- webinars or partner motions.

5. Define channel strategy
For each recommended channel, provide:
- role in funnel,
- why it matters,
- required assets,
- recommended cadence,
- and realistic success expectations.

6. Define asset stack
Specify the minimum viable marketing asset system, including:
- homepage,
- solution pages,
- founder LinkedIn engine,
- SEO article clusters,
- one-pager,
- demo support narrative,
- sales deck,
- proof assets,
- retargeting creative,
- campaign landing pages if relevant.

7. Define phases
Create a phased GTM plan such as:
- phase 1: foundations,
- phase 2: visibility and trust,
- phase 3: conversion optimization,
- phase 4: scale experiments.

8. Define metrics and feedback loops
For each phase or channel, identify:
- what should be measured,
- what leading indicators matter,
- what failure signals to watch,
- and what to improve next.

==================================================
DEFAULT CHANNEL PRIORITY LOGIC
==================================================

Unless there is stronger evidence otherwise, use this default priority order:
1. Positioning and message clarity
2. Homepage and conversion copy
3. Founder-led LinkedIn content
4. SEO and search-intent content
5. Sales enablement assets
6. Retargeting
7. LinkedIn paid campaigns
8. Email nurture
9. Events, webinars, and partnerships
10. Experimental or guerrilla tactics
11. Facebook or Instagram only for a defined support role

==================================================
OUTPUT FORMAT
==================================================

Return the output in this structure:

1. GTM Context Summary
2. Assumptions and Constraints
3. Primary and Secondary ICPs
4. Buyer and Stakeholder Roles
5. Recommended Positioning Lead
6. GTM Motion Summary
7. Channel Priority Table
8. Required Asset Stack
9. 90-Day Plan
10. 6-Month Expansion Plan
11. Messaging by Funnel Stage
12. Risks and Failure Modes
13. KPI and Measurement Framework
14. Recommendations for Immediate Next Steps
15. Open Questions

==================================================
QUALITY BAR
==================================================

The final output should feel like an actionable B2B SaaS go-to-market operating plan, not a motivational marketing memo.
It should be specific enough for a founder or AI system to turn into a real execution roadmap.
```

## Recommended use

Use this file after positioning is at least partially defined. It works best when there is already some clarity around audience, pains, and strategic messaging.
