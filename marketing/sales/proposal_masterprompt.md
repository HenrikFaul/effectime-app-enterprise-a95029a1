# Proposal Master Prompt

## Purpose
Guide for writing customized Effectime proposals. A proposal is sent after a qualified demo conversation to give the prospect what they need to make a decision and, if applicable, get internal approval.

---

## When to send a proposal

Only after:
- A discovery conversation where you understand their specific situation
- The prospect has seen the product (demo)
- There is genuine buying intent (not just curiosity)

Do not send proposals to cold prospects. A proposal is a closing tool, not a cold outreach tool.

---

## Proposal structure

### 1. Executive summary (½ page)

Start with their words, not yours. Summarize the problem THEY described.

> "Based on our conversation, [Company name]'s main challenge is [specific pain they described — use their language]. You're currently managing [X process] through [current method], which is causing [specific consequence they mentioned]."

This tells them you listened. It builds instant trust.

### 2. Proposed solution (1 page)

Tailored, not generic. Connect specific Effectime features to their specific pain.

> "We recommend starting with [2–3 specific features]. Here's how each addresses what you described:
> - [Feature A]: [Their specific pain] → [Specific outcome]
> - [Feature B]: [Their specific pain] → [Specific outcome]"

### 3. Implementation timeline (½ page)

Simple 3-phase timeline. No project management language.

> "Phase 1 (Week 1): Workspace setup, offices configured, admin trained.
> Phase 2 (Week 2): Team invited, leave policies activated, first requests processed.
> Phase 3 (Week 3–4): Running independently. Support available if needed."

### 4. Pricing (½ page)

Transparent, simple, no surprises.

Present the plan that fits their situation. Show the per-person-per-month and total monthly cost. One line about what's included. One line about the free trial.

### 5. ROI framing (¼ page)

Don't build a complex calculator. One or two concrete numbers.

> "Based on what you told us, if this saves your HR manager 3 hours per week, that's [€X/month] in recovered time — at [their hourly estimate]. The tool costs [price]. That's [N×] return."

### 6. Proof element (¼ page)

One customer story most similar to their situation. 3–5 sentences.

### 7. Next step (very short)

One clear next step. Not a menu.

> "If this looks right, the easiest next step is to start the 14-day trial. I'll walk you through the setup personally — takes about 10 minutes. Reply to confirm and I'll send you the link."

---

## Proposal tone guide

- Confident, not desperate
- Specific to them, not generic
- Short — maximum 3 pages for an SMB proposal
- No legal boilerplate or enterprise contract language (that's for later)
- Written in the same conversational register as your conversation — not a formal document voice

### Page

- Make a good first impression on the cover page.
- Landing page and thank you page.

### Send

- Sign and send documents for legally binding e-signatures.
- Send Your Emails at the Correct Time.
- Avoid sending emails late at night or on weekends.
- Send at least 3-4 follow-ups spaced a few days apart.

### Specific

- Edit and personalize fillable forms for your specific needs.
- Modify the wording to fit the recipient’s industry and specific challenges.
- Mention their company, industry, or a specific challenge they face.

### Pain

- Identify their pain points and tailor your email accordingly.
- Identify common pain points within their industry.

### General Principles

- Qualify and uncover the needs of the client or internal stakeholder during a discovery session.
- Create a plan of action, your vision for what must be done to achieve their goals.
- Detail the findings of your discovery session with the executive summary.
- Outline their goals and challenges and set the stage for your solutions.
- Expand on the strategies and tactics you plan to use to reach their goals.
- Provide a summary where you clearly lay out the scope of work.
- Detail how you'll measure success in the metrics section.
- If it's for a client, convince them that you're the right agency for the job.
