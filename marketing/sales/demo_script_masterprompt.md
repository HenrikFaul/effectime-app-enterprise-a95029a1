# Demo Script Master Prompt

## Purpose
Scripts for live and recorded Effectime product demos. Three variants: HR persona, Ops/manager persona, Owner/founder persona. Each follows the same structure but with different pain focus and feature emphasis.

---

## Demo principles

1. **Start with discovery, not the product.** Ask 1–2 questions before showing anything.
2. **Show the problem being solved, not the feature.** Lead every screen with: "This is the moment where [pain] gets fixed."
3. **Use realistic data.** Never show empty or clearly fake demo data.
4. **Intercept objections during the demo**, not after.
5. **Close to a next step**, not to "any questions?"

---

## Opening discovery questions (choose 1–2)

- "Before I show you anything — what's the most frustrating part of [leave management / scheduling / coverage] for you right now?"
- "What does the process look like today when an employee requests time off?"
- "How do you currently know if you have enough coverage on any given day?"
- "How many locations are you managing, and do they have the same setup?"

**Listen actively.** Adjust the demo path based on their answer.

---

## Demo path A: HR manager (leave and attendance focus)

**Screen 1: Leave request from employee view**
"This is what an employee sees. They submit a request in 30 seconds. Let me show you what you see on the other side..."

**Screen 2: Manager approval view with balance**
"You see the request, the current balance, who else is on leave that day, and coverage status. One click to approve or reject — with a note if needed."

**Screen 3: Leave calendar, team view**
"This is the view you check before every team meeting. Who's in, who's out, and whether you're covered. No spreadsheet, no calling around."

**Screen 4: Attendance and export**
"At the end of the month — export to CSV for payroll in two clicks. Clean, accurate, ready."

**Intercept:** "Does this match how your approval process currently works?"

---

## Demo path B: Operations / scheduling manager (coverage focus)

**Screen 1: Coverage rules configuration**
"You set the rules for each location: which positions or skills need to be present on which days. You set this once."

**Screen 2: Coverage gap alert**
"When leave creates a gap — the system shows it automatically. You see it before it becomes a problem."

**Screen 3: Multi-site view**
"You're seeing all your locations at once. Different rules per site, same unified view."

**Intercept:** "How are you currently checking if you're covered at each location?"

---

## Demo path C: Business owner (control and visibility focus)

**Screen 1: Workspace dashboard**
"This is your overview. Who's in today, who's out, any pending requests, coverage status. You have this without asking anyone."

**Screen 2: Reporting dashboard**
"Any report you'd normally ask HR to build for you — available here in one click. Leave by department. Attendance trends. Coverage history."

**Screen 3: Admin controls**
"You can manage who sees what, which locations have which rules, and how the whole system is structured. Without an IT department."

**Intercept:** "Is visibility across the whole business something you're currently missing?"

---

## Demo close

Do NOT ask: "So, what do you think?"

Instead: "Based on what you told me about [their specific pain], does this address that? [Wait for answer] — Great. The easiest next step is a free trial. I can walk you through the 10-minute setup right now, or you can start it yourself. Which would you prefer?"

### Focus

- Focus on key opportunities and provide strategic guidance and coaching to make sure deals are actively progressing towards close.
- Keep your focus on the end goals and know how those action items will get you there.
- Focus on their desired outcomes and the most common speed bumps rather than technical functionality.

### Manager

- These meetings help build strong relationships between the manager and the employee.
- Establish the product manager as a strategic partner to sales, not just a technical resource.

### Persona

- They improve the sales rep's accountability and help them grow professionally and personally.
- Start the conversation with personal questions like how they're doing, gradually moving towards growth and work-related questions.

### Demo

- Using real-world customer goals and pain points as the foundation for the demo.
- Showcase real-world examples and success stories and make sure your demo data looks like a real customer environment instead of cryptic test data.
