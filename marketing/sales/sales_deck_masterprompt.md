# Sales Deck Master Prompt

## Purpose
Guide for creating the Effectime sales presentation. Used in live demos, recorded walkthroughs, and investor conversations. Follows the narrative house: problem → hero → system → proof → next step.

---

## Sales deck structure (12–15 slides)

### Slide 1: Opener — A day in the life of coordination chaos
**Goal:** Immediate recognition from the prospect. Open with a specific scenario they've lived.
**Talking points:** "Picture this Monday morning..." [use the scenario most relevant to their company type]
**Visual:** Minimalist illustration or text-only. No Effectime logo yet.
**What NOT to say:** "Effectime is a leading HR platform..." (kills trust immediately)

### Slide 2: The problem is bigger than it looks
**Goal:** Show the true scope of what scattered coordination costs.
**Content:** 3 costs of informal people management: time cost, error cost, coordination cost.
**Visual:** Simple stat cards or before/after breakdown.

### Slide 3: What they've tried (and why it didn't work)
**Goal:** Validate their journey and build empathy.
**Content:** Spreadsheets → too manual. Enterprise HRIS → too complex. Nothing → now you're here.
**Talking point:** "You're not here because you ignored the problem. You're here because the right tool didn't exist for your size."

### Slide 4: What Effectime is (and isn't)
**Goal:** Position clearly, not as a feature list.
**Content:** One sentence. "Effectime is a workforce coordination system for businesses that are too big for spreadsheets and too small for enterprise HR software."
**What it's not:** Not an ATS. Not payroll software. Not a project management tool.

### Slide 5: Leave management
**Goal:** Demonstrate the core leave management capability.
**Show:** Request → approval → balance update — in one screen.
**Talking point:** "Every request is tracked, every balance is accurate, and nothing lives in a chat thread."

### Slide 6: Coverage rules
**Goal:** Show coverage gap prevention.
**Show:** Coverage rule configuration → gap alert → manager response.
**Talking point:** "You set the rules once. The system flags when they're not met — before the problem happens."

### Slide 7: Multi-site management
**Goal:** Show multi-location capability.
**Show:** Multiple offices, member assignments, different coverage rules per site.
**Talking point:** "One workspace. All locations. Every manager sees only what they need."

### Slide 8: Time & attendance
**Goal:** Show attendance tracking and payroll-ready export.
**Show:** Day editor, export button, clean CSV output.
**Talking point:** "No more building payroll reports by hand. Two clicks, done."

### Slide 9: Proof slide
**Goal:** Evidence that it works.
**Content:** 1–2 customer outcomes (quantified if available). "A 45-person company saved 6 hours/week..."
**Talking point:** Let the outcomes speak. Don't explain them.

### Slide 10: Implementation
**Goal:** Remove the fear of complexity.
**Content:** 3-phase timeline. Week 1: setup. Week 2: team onboarded. Week 3: running independently.
**Talking point:** "We've done this with companies your size. It's not a 6-month project."

### Slide 11: Pricing
**Goal:** Transparent, non-defensive.
**Show:** Simple pricing table.
**Talking point:** "Based on what you told me, you'd be on [plan]. That's [price]. Any questions before I show you how to get started?"

### Slide 12: Next step
**Goal:** Clear call to action with no friction.
**Options:** "Try free for 14 days — I'll walk you through setup" OR "Let's start the trial now — 10 minutes and your workspace is ready."
**What NOT to do:** End with "So what do you think?" or "Any thoughts?" — offer a concrete path.

---

## What NOT to say in a sales conversation

- "Our platform leverages AI to optimize your workforce" (hype)
- "We're different because we're more user-friendly" (every product claims this)
- "We have all the features you need" (never lead with features)
- "Let me walk you through our product" (walk them through THEIR problem first)
