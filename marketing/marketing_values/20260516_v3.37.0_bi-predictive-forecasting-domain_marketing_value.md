Business Intelligence Predictive Analytics and Forecasting Domain — v3.37.0

## 2. User-Language Description

Effectime now has a structured predictive intelligence layer that moves HR and operations from reactive problem-solving to proactive decision-making. Instead of discovering a coverage gap after it has disrupted scheduling, or recognising a wellbeing decline after turnover has begun, the system projects where metrics are heading — with explicit confidence levels, seasonal context, and the specific dates by which interventions must be initiated. Every forecast is bounded, version-verified, and tied to a specific decision.

---

## 3. Problem It Replaces

**Previously**: Workforce problems were diagnosed after they manifested — understaffing became visible after overtime spiked, wellbeing crises became visible after resignations, compliance risk became visible after violations. Forecasting, if done at all, was manual, inconsistent, and untied to a decision timeline.

**Now**: Eight domain-specific forecasting prompts with a master methodology controller produce bounded, version-aware, decay-dated projections for shift demand, labor cost, wellbeing trajectory, departure risk, compliance risk, and capacity-demand gaps — with intervention lead time analysis that tells decision-makers exactly how long they have to act.

---

## 4. Primary Beneficiary Personas

**Primary**: Effectime internal — AI agents, product analytics, customer success teams using predictive outputs to support workspace decision-making.

**Downstream value**:
- **HR directors and operations managers** who need to make headcount decisions weeks before the coverage gap opens, not days after.
- **Founders and CFOs** who need labor cost projections and overtime budget risk assessments before the end-of-period overspend is confirmed.
- **People managers** who need to know which teams are approaching burnout with enough time to intervene during the wellbeing recovery window.
- **Customer success** who need workspace-level risk signals to prioritise proactive outreach before churn.

## 5. Marketing-Ready Claim

"Most workforce software tells you what happened. Effectime tells you what's about to happen — and when you need to act to change it. Coverage gaps are projected 6–8 weeks ahead with an intervention window. Burnout timelines are estimated from wellbeing velocity, not gut feel. Labor cost overruns are flagged at the burn rate inflection point, not at period-end. This is what it means to manage a workforce proactively."

---

## 6. Claim Types Enabled

- **Proactive management**: Coverage gap forecasting with intervention lead time analysis — "you have 3 weeks to act before the gap becomes critical."
- **Financial control**: Labor cost burn rate index with escalation thresholds — overspend is visible before it compounds.
- **People risk prevention**: Wellbeing trajectory and burnout timeline estimation from survey velocity — intervention window is quantified, not vague.
- **Retention intelligence**: Departure risk scoring from 9 behavioural signals in Effectime data — risk is classified before the resignation, not after.
- **Decision support**: Scenario modeling with decision-specific impact chains — "here's the cost difference between hiring 3 vs. 5 people, accounting for onboarding ramp."
- **Seasonal intelligence**: 16 documented seasonal patterns across 5 metric domains — forecasts that know December attendance is always lower aren't surprised by it.

## 7. Content Angles

1. **"You found out too late"** — LinkedIn / thought leadership on the cost of reactive HR management. The framing: every HR crisis had a 6-week warning that nobody read. Use the capacity-demand gap forecasting cascade chain as the illustration.

2. **"The 3 numbers every HR leader should track before the quarter ends"** — educational content built around the burn rate index, wellbeing velocity, and marketplace fill rate as the three leading indicators of Q-end operational crisis. Effectime's forecasting layer makes them visible.

3. **"What a wellbeing score decline is actually telling you"** — content piece on velocity vs. point-in-time reading of wellbeing data. Uses the trajectory forecasting framework (seasonally adjusted velocity → burnout timeline → intervention window) as the analytical backbone.

4. **"Scenario planning for shift managers"** — practical guide on how operations managers can use what-if modeling before making scheduling or incentive decisions, not after. Positions Effectime as decision-support infrastructure, not a data recording tool.

5. **"The difference between a coverage report and a coverage forecast"** — positioning content distinguishing backward-looking HR analytics (current state) from forward-looking planning (gap forecast, intervention window, cascade risk). Strong differentiation from standard HR dashboards.
