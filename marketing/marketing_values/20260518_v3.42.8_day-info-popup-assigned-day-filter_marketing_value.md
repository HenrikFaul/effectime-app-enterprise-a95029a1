# Marketing Value — v3.42.8

**Feature slug:** day-info-popup-assigned-day-filter  
**Date:** 2026-05-18

---

## User-language problem solved

**For the employee (My Portal / time-attendance view):**
"I tap on a day in my monthly calendar but I have no idea what I actually recorded — I have to open the day editor just to see my own schedule."

Now: a single tap on any day with data shows a clean summary — what hours, what location, my availability flag, on-call duties — without entering edit mode.

**For the My Portal open-shifts panel:**
"Even though I'm already assigned to a shift on Thursday, the open-shifts panel still shows me another open position for Thursday and lets me try to claim it — I only find out I can't when the error pops up."

Now: shifts on days the employee is already scheduled are silently filtered out. The panel only ever shows actionable options.

---

## Personas that benefit

- **Employee / Individual contributor** — reads their own monthly schedule with zero friction; no false-positive open shifts cluttering their marketplace view
- **Manager / HR** — reduced confusion tickets ("why does it show me shifts I can't take?"); employees see a cleaner, more trustworthy system

---

## Marketing claims enabled

- "Instant day summary — tap any calendar day to see your schedule, location, and availability at a glance."
- "Smart shift filtering — the marketplace only shows you shifts you can actually take."
- "No dead ends — the app never surfaces actions that will just result in an error."

---

## Content angles

- **UX polish story:** "We removed friction at every tap" — the day info popup is a zero-effort way to consult your own schedule without entering edit mode.
- **Trust / reliability angle:** "Effectime only shows you what's relevant" — filtering unavailable shifts builds trust that the UI is on the employee's side.
- **Mobile-first:** The popup is compact (max-w-sm) and built for quick mobile glance — strong angle for the mobile app announcement.
