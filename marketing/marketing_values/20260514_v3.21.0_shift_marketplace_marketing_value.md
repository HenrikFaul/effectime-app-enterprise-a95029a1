# v3.21.0 — Shift Marketplace — Marketing Value

## One-sentence description

Peer-to-peer shift trading with eligibility-aware matching — when an
employee needs to swap a shift, qualified colleagues are auto-notified
and the first to accept triggers manager approval (or auto-approves
under workspace policy).

## Personas helped

- **Frontline workers** — flexibility to swap a shift without phone
  trees, group-chat scramble, or 6am sick-call panic.
- **Managers** — 60–70% reduction in coverage-related interruptions
  (Shiftboard benchmark). Approve a trade from a single click instead
  of orchestrating it manually.
- **Operations / scheduling leads** — eligibility helper enforces no
  same-day conflicts, no approved-leave conflicts, no own-shift
  trades. Skill matching is the next polish PR.

## Claim types enabled

- **Operational efficiency** — concrete time savings for managers.
- **Employee satisfaction** — flexibility is a top driver of frontline
  retention. The strategy doc cites Homebase's coverage feature as
  their #1 retention driver.
- **Industry penetration** — retail, healthcare, F&B, hospitality —
  all high-shift-density sectors where this is a procurement
  requirement.

## Content angles

1. **Customer case study** — Pick an existing retail or healthcare
   workspace; document the manager-time-saved metric after 30 days of
   marketplace use.
2. **Sales enablement** — One-pager comparing Effectime's marketplace
   to When I Work and Homebase, emphasizing the **server-validated
   eligibility** (Effectime checks on the DB; competitors check on
   the client).
3. **LinkedIn post** — "How we turned 6am 'who can cover for me?' panic
   into a 30-second peer-to-peer marketplace."
