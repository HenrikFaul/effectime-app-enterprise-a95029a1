# Brand Safety Rules

## Purpose
Rules for maintaining Effectime's brand integrity across all channels and situations. Covers: claim discipline, competitor behavior, crisis protocol, content association, and moderation rules.

---

## Tone in negative situations

### Responding to critical reviews or complaints
- Acknowledge the experience specifically — never dismiss
- Do not be defensive in public; take the conversation private when possible
- Offer a concrete next step (talk to support, schedule a call)
- Do not promise things that aren't certain
- Never delete legitimate critical feedback unless it violates platform community rules

### Competitor comparisons (unsolicited)
- Do not initiate attacks on competitors in public content
- If a prospect asks for a comparison, provide it factually and let the product speak
- Do not celebrate competitor failures publicly
- Comparison page content should be neutral-tone (factual differences, not attacks)

---

## What not to claim

- **Compliance guarantees:** Do not say "fully GDPR compliant" or "legally compliant" without specific legal backing. Use: "designed with GDPR principles" or "consult your legal team for compliance assessment."
- **ROI promises:** Do not promise specific ROI percentages without evidence. Use: "companies report saving X hours/week" only when documented.
- **Uptime/availability:** Do not make uptime promises in marketing without an SLA in place.
- **Integration completeness:** Do not claim an integration "works with" a tool unless tested and functional.
- **Security claims:** Do not make absolute security claims. Use: "data encrypted in transit and at rest" if verifiable.

---

## Content association rules

Do not publish content that:
- Engages with politically divisive topics unrelated to the business
- Uses humor at the expense of any professional group, nationality, or identity
- Implies that poor HR management is caused by personal failure (it's a systems problem, not a people problem)
- Makes light of workplace accidents, compliance failures, or employee safety issues
- Exaggerates the pain of the status quo to the point of making buyers feel bad

---

## Social media moderation rules

**Comments on Effectime's posts:**
- Respond to genuine questions within 24 business hours
- Thank positive comments briefly, don't over-engage
- Ignore trolling; do not engage
- Hide (not delete) spam or irrelevant promotional comments
- Delete: hate speech, threats, personal attacks on individuals

**Founder personal account:**
- Founder represents Effectime even on personal posts in the same professional context
- Political opinions unrelated to business: keep off professional channels
- Business results and learnings: appropriate to share

---

## Press and analyst mentions

- Do not confirm or deny revenue numbers or user counts to press unless strategically approved
- Product roadmap: do not commit to public timelines in press statements
- Acquisition rumors: no comment without legal review
- Positive coverage: repost with attribution; do not editorialize

---

## Content takedown criteria

Remove content immediately if:
- It contains factually incorrect product claims that could mislead buyers
- It accidentally includes confidential customer data
- It was published in error (wrong timing, wrong audience, wrong content)
- Legal or compliance issue is identified

For all other requests: review before removing; not every negative reaction warrants takedown.
