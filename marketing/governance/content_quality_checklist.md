# Content Quality Checklist for Effectime Marketing Assets

## Purpose

Use this checklist to review marketing copy, content, campaign assets, and web pages before publishing.

## Checklist

### 1. Positioning and clarity
- Does the asset explain what Effectime is in outcome language, not just feature language?
- Does it avoid generic HR/SAAS clichés?
- Is the target audience clear?
- Is the core problem easy to understand within a few seconds?

### 2. Audience and intent
- Is the content clearly written for a specific role, buyer, or use case?
- Does it match the awareness stage?
- Does it avoid mixing too many audiences in one message?

### 3. Message architecture
- Is there a single primary message?
- Are supporting messages aligned with the primary message?
- Are benefits translated into operational outcomes?
- Is there a sensible progression from pain to outcome to proof?

### 4. Proof and credibility
- Does the asset include proof, examples, or credible specifics?
- Does it avoid empty claims?
- If metrics are used, are they believable and contextualized?
- If proof is not numeric, is it still concrete and relevant?

### 5. UX and scanability
- Is the structure easy to scan?
- Are headers useful and descriptive?
- Are paragraphs short enough?
- Is the CTA easy to find?
- Is the visual hierarchy clear?

### 6. Channel fit
- Is the asset appropriate for the channel where it will appear?
- If it is for LinkedIn, does it feel native and useful?
- If it is for email, is it concise enough?
- If it is for the website, does it support conversion?

### 7. CTA and next step
- Is there a clear next step?
- Is the CTA matched to the audience stage?
- Is the CTA too aggressive, too vague, or appropriately timed?

### 8. Risk and consistency
- Does the content stay consistent with Effectime’s positioning?
- Does it avoid overpromising?
- Does it avoid channel mismatch?
- Does it avoid unsupported claims?

### 9. Visual and design quality
- If visual elements are included, do they support the message?
- Is the design clean and credible?
- Is there any decorative clutter that reduces clarity?
- Does the visual style match the brand and channel?

### 10. Final decision
- Is the asset strong enough to publish?
- What is the one biggest weakness?
- What must be fixed before release?
- What is the one thing that would most improve the asset if time allowed?

## Usage

Review each item before publishing. If any critical item fails, revise the asset before release.

## Quality bar

The goal is not perfection. The goal is to prevent vague, off-brand, low-credibility, or hard-to-scan assets from shipping.
