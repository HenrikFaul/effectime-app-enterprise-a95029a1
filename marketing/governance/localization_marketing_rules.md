# Localization Marketing Rules

## Purpose
Rules for adapting Effectime marketing content to different language markets. The product is fully localized for 5 locales. Marketing must match. This file defines tone, terminology, channel, and review rules per market.

---

## Active marketing markets

| Market | Language | Priority | Notes |
|--------|----------|----------|-------|
| Hungary | Hungarian (HU) | Primary | Founder's home market; deepest product and market knowledge |
| Czech Republic | Czech (CZ) | Tier 1 | Strong SMB market; familiar with SaaS adoption |
| Slovakia | Slovak (SK) | Tier 1 | Adjacent to CZ; different language, similar buyer profile |
| Poland | Polish (PL) | Tier 2 | Larger market; requires more investment to penetrate |
| International | English (EN) | Tier 2 | UK, international tech companies, English-speaking markets |

---

## Hungarian market (primary)

**B2B tone:** Formal address ("Ön") is expected in professional B2B contexts. Informal tone ("te") is acceptable for founder-voice LinkedIn content but should be used deliberately.

**HR/ops vocabulary:**
- "szabadságkezelés" — leave management (preferred over "szabadság adminisztráció")
- "jelenléti nyilvántartás" — attendance tracking
- "lefedettség" — coverage (in the scheduling/staffing sense)
- "telephely" — office/site (multi-site use)
- "üzletvezető" — store/site manager

**Channel notes:** LinkedIn is growing in HU for B2B. Facebook groups for SMB owners/HR professionals remain relevant for awareness. Google search in Hungarian should be prioritized for SEO.

**Cultural note:** Direct pain-calling works well in HU market ("Ha Excel-ben kezeled a szabadságokat, ez számodra szól"). Avoid overclaiming; Hungarian B2B buyers are skeptical of hype.

---

## Czech market

**B2B tone:** Professional, direct. Czech B2B is similar to German-speaking markets — efficiency and reliability matter more than aspirational language.

**HR/ops vocabulary:**
- "správa dovolených" — leave management
- "docházka" — attendance (the standard term)
- "pokrytí" — coverage
- "pobočka" — branch/office

**Channel notes:** LinkedIn strong for B2B. Czech-language SEO is underserved in HR/ops space — opportunity for Effectime.

---

## Slovak market

**B2B tone:** Similar to Czech. Slovak B2B buyers appreciate straight-talking, practical communication.

**HR/ops vocabulary:**
- "správa dovoleniek" — leave management
- "dochádzka" — attendance
- "pokrytie" — coverage
- "pobočka" — branch/office

**Channel notes:** Small market; LinkedIn organic + content can reach a significant proportion of the target ICP. Slovak-specific SEO is very low competition.

---

## Polish market

**B2B tone:** Polish B2B can be more formal than CZ/SK. Use pan/pani formality in written copy unless specifically testing informal approach.

**HR/ops vocabulary:**
- "zarządzanie urlopami" — leave management
- "ewidencja czasu pracy" — attendance/time tracking (formal)
- "obsada stanowisk" — staffing/coverage
- "oddział" / "biuro" — branch/office

**Channel notes:** LinkedIn is growing fast in PL. Polish-language content marketing for HR/ops is a real opportunity. More investment required vs. CZ/SK due to market size.

---

## English (international) market

**B2B tone:** Conversational, direct, outcome-led. US-style marketing language (more casual, more benefit-led) works better than formal European tone.

**Channel notes:** LinkedIn primary. SEO competition is high in English; focus on specific, low-competition operational topics. Consider niche communities (Ops/HR Slack communities, Reddit r/humanresources).

---

## What must NOT be machine-translated without review

- Hero headlines (must be reviewed by a native B2B professional, not just a translator)
- CTA button copy (tone and directness varies significantly by market)
- Pricing page copy (legally and commercially sensitive)
- Customer testimonials (must be genuine, native-language quotes)
- Email subject lines (humor, idioms, and urgency signals translate poorly)
- Any compliance-adjacent claims

**Acceptable for machine translation with light review:**
- Feature descriptions
- Blog article drafts (must be reviewed for tone and terminology)
- UI help text (review for terminology consistency with product localization)

---

## Translation review checklist

Before publishing localized marketing content:
- [ ] Native speaker reviewed for natural tone (not just grammatical accuracy)
- [ ] HR/ops terminology matches product's own localized terminology
- [ ] CTA tested for click-motivation in native language
- [ ] Any claims verified as accurate in the target market's regulatory context
- [ ] Cultural references checked (idioms, examples, company names used are relevant to that market)
