# Website Art Direction

## Purpose
Art direction for Effectime website design. Covers hero section, feature sections, social proof, CTAs, and mobile. Provides AI image generation prompts where relevant.

---

## Homepage hero section

**Visual metaphor:** Before/after contrast — organized vs. chaotic coordination. The hero should visually communicate clarity, not complexity.

**Recommended approaches:**
1. Clean product screenshot showing the main dashboard with realistic team data (not empty state)
2. Split composition: left = clean, organized (Effectime), right = messy (message threads, spreadsheet chaos)
3. Abstract: clean geometric lines showing connection and flow (conservative but professional)

**AI image prompt (option 1 — product screenshot hero):**
> "Professional B2B SaaS website hero background, deep navy and white color scheme, clean minimal office environment, no people, subtle light gradient, high-end technology product aesthetic, lots of negative space for UI overlay, photorealistic, professional corporate photography style"

**AI image prompt (option 2 — operational clarity metaphor):**
> "Overhead view of organized modern office desk, calendar and schedule neatly arranged, clean and minimal, daylight, no people, professional business photography, neutral colors with slight blue-gray tones, 16:9 format"

## Feature section art direction

Each feature section should have: a screenshot or UI illustration + an annotation callout pointing to the key action/outcome.

**Rules:**
- Screenshots must show realistic data (real names, real dates, real numbers — use realistic dummy data)
- Callout text should name the OUTCOME, not the feature: "Gap flagged 2 weeks ahead" not "Coverage rules engine"
- Consistent annotation style: small pill-shaped callout, brand color, clean sans-serif text

---

## Social proof / testimonial section

**Layout:** Card-based, 2–3 testimonials visible. Each card: quote, role/company type, small company indicator (not logo if not permitted).

**Visual style:** Light background, subtle shadow, brand accent for quote marks. No stock photos of "the customer" unless real.

---

## Call-to-action sections

**Primary CTA buttons:**
- Background: brand primary color (deep navy or brand blue)
- Text: white, semibold
- Minimum size: large enough to be tapped on mobile
- Clear space: 32px+ above and below

**Secondary CTA:**
- Outlined or ghost button style
- Same size as primary

**Positioning:** CTA always visible above the fold. Repeat CTA at end of every major section.

---

## Mobile art direction

Same visual hierarchy as desktop — do not strip content to the point of losing context. Key changes for mobile:
- Hero: screenshot moves below headline (not beside)
- Feature sections: single column, screenshots full-width
- CTA: sticky bottom bar on mobile for trial signup
- Navigation: hamburger menu; primary CTA always visible
