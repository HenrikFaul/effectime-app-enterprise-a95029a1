# Brand Visual System

## Purpose
Effectime visual identity guidelines for AI image/design prompts and human designers. Defines style direction, color, typography, photography, and what to avoid. Read before producing any visual asset.

---

## Visual identity direction

**Core aesthetic:** Professional, modern, operational clarity. Not cold/corporate, not startup-trendy, not consumer-app casual.

The visual system should communicate: "This is a serious tool built by people who understand operational work — not a lifestyle app."

---

## Color direction

**Primary palette:**
- Deep navy / professional dark blue — authority, trust, structure
- Clean white / off-white — clarity, space, readability
- Medium blue-gray — secondary backgrounds, neutral sections

**Accent colors (functional, not decorative):**
- Amber / yellow — warnings, important notices, pending states
- Emerald green — approved, confirmed, positive status
- Rose/red — rejected, error, urgent attention
- Sky blue — informational, selected, active state

**Rule:** Accent colors carry meaning. Do not use them decoratively or interchangeably. The color system should be legible even to colorblind users (never rely on color alone to convey state).

---

## Typography direction

**Preferred typefaces:**
- Headlines: Clean, geometric sans-serif (Inter, DM Sans, or equivalent)
- Body: Highly readable sans-serif at multiple sizes
- Data/numbers: Tabular figures for alignment in tables and dashboards

**Rules:**
- No decorative or script fonts
- Headline weight: bold (700) or semibold (600) — never thin for headlines
- Body weight: regular (400) — never all-caps for body text
- Line height: 1.5–1.6 for readability

---

## Photography direction

**Approved photography styles:**
- Real workplace environments (offices, desks, meeting rooms) — not abstract
- Small team interactions — 2–5 people, natural and focused, not staged
- Screens showing work being done — annotated with purpose
- Close-ups of operational moments: someone checking a schedule, reviewing a report

**Banned photography approaches:**
- Group of smiling people pointing at a whiteboard
- Generic "business handshake" or "team huddle" stock photos
- Overly diverse but obviously staged "diversity marketing" imagery
- Abstract tech visuals (glowing circuits, DNA spirals, floating data)
- Any image that looks like it's trying to be a startup

---

## Icon and illustration direction

- Clean, functional icon style (line icons or simple filled icons — consistent)
- No 3D icons, no skeuomorphic style
- Illustration used sparingly — only for complex process explanations
- Diagram style: clean flowcharts, simple hierarchy charts for org/process

---

## Do not use

- Gradient overload (maximum one subtle gradient per design)
- Drop shadows on everything
- Multiple fonts on one page
- Busy, decorative patterns as backgrounds
- "Flat illustration" human characters (cartoon people)
- Fake phone/laptop mockups with clearly wrong UI
