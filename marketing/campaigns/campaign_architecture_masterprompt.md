# Campaign Architecture Master Prompt

## Purpose
Master framework for planning Effectime demand generation campaigns. Defines campaign types, anatomy, brief template, and budget principles. Read before planning any campaign.

---

## Campaign types

| Type | Goal | Audience state | Primary metric |
|------|------|----------------|---------------|
| Awareness | Get ICP to know the problem category | Cold, no prior contact | Qualified impressions, engagement |
| Demand capture | Convert active searchers | Warm, actively looking | Demo requests, trial signups |
| Nurture | Move consideration-stage leads to intent | Known, not yet ready | Trial signup, content click |
| Re-engagement | Reactivate lapsed trials or cold leads | Previously interested | Return visit, trial reactivation |
| Product launch | Drive attention and signups for new feature | Existing + new audience | Feature adoption, signups |

---

## Campaign anatomy

Every campaign needs these elements defined before any creative or copy is produced:

1. **Audience** — who specifically (job title, company size, market, behavior signal)
2. **Message** — the one thing we want this audience to believe or feel after seeing the campaign
3. **Proof** — what evidence supports the message
4. **Creative direction** — what the visual/copy will look and sound like
5. **Channel** — where it runs and why
6. **Landing destination** — where we send traffic and what we expect them to do
7. **CTA** — what specific action we want
8. **Follow-up** — what happens after the CTA (nurture sequence, sales touchpoint, etc.)
9. **Success metric** — one primary KPI
10. **Budget and run duration** — how much, for how long

## Budget allocation principles (B2B SMB SaaS, early stage)

At early stage (< €5k/month marketing budget):
- 50% — content and SEO (highest long-term ROI)
- 30% — LinkedIn paid (most precise ICP targeting)
- 20% — retargeting (recaptures warm audience cheaply)

At growth stage (€5k–20k/month):
- 30% — content and SEO
- 35% — LinkedIn paid
- 15% — retargeting
- 10% — email automation and tools
- 10% — experiments (new channels, formats)

**Rule:** Never run paid campaigns to a cold audience until the website converts at > 3% of qualified visitors. Paid traffic amplifies conversion, it doesn't fix a broken funnel.
