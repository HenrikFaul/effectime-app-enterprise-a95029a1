# Launch Campaign Master Prompt

## Purpose
Playbook for launching new Effectime features or the product itself to new markets. Covers pre-launch, launch day, and post-launch phases with specific asset lists and messaging guidance.

---

## When to run a launch campaign

- Major new module (not incremental improvements)
- Entry into a new geographic market
- Significant pricing or packaging change
- Milestone achievement worth sharing (first 100 customers, first international customer, etc.)

---

## Phase 1: Pre-launch (2–3 weeks before)

**Goal:** Build anticipation with the existing audience; tease the problem being solved without revealing the feature.

**Assets:**
- 2–3 LinkedIn posts about the pain the feature solves (no product mention)
- 1 email to list: "We've been building something for a problem we keep hearing about. More next week."
- Social "what's coming" tease with visual (no product reveal, just problem language)

**Rule:** Pre-launch content should be so good that people are interested even if Effectime never existed. It's education-first, product-second.

---

## Phase 2: Launch day

**Goal:** Announce clearly, concisely, and with proof.

**Asset checklist:**
- [ ] Founder LinkedIn post: personal announcement with specific problem + what changed
- [ ] Brand LinkedIn post with visual/screenshot
- [ ] Email to full list: what launched, who it's for, trial link
- [ ] Product update email to existing customers (separate, warmer tone)
- [ ] Feature page or landing page live on website
- [ ] marketing_values entry created in `marketing/marketing_values/`

**Launch day post template (founder voice):**
> "We shipped [feature] today.
> The problem it solves: [1 sentence, user-language].
> Before this, customers had to [old way].
> Now: [new way, one sentence].
> If you manage [target audience], try it: [link]."

---

## Phase 3: Post-launch (weeks 2–4)

**Goal:** Deepen engagement, collect early proof, start amplifying with paid.

**Week 2:**
- Follow-up LinkedIn post: early reaction or use-case story
- Email to non-openers from launch email (resend with different subject line)
- LinkedIn paid campaign if budget available: push to feature landing page

**Week 3–4:**
- First case/outcome story from an early adopter
- SEO article on the problem the feature solves (long-term demand capture)
- Retargeting ad for feature page visitors who didn't sign up

---

## Post-launch measurement

Review at 30 days:
- Trial signups attributed to launch
- Feature adoption rate among existing customers
- LinkedIn post reach and engagement vs. baseline
- Email open and click rate vs. previous campaigns
- Any earned media or community shares

Document findings in the marketing_values entry for the feature.
