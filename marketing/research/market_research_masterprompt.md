# Market Research Masterprompt — Workforce Management SaaS

## Purpose
Use this prompt to guide AI-assisted research into the workforce management SaaS market. The output should inform Effectime's positioning, channel investment, and messaging priorities. Run this research quarterly or when entering a new segment or geography.

---

## Research Mission
Map the workforce management SaaS market for SMB-to-mid-market companies (20–500 employees) across Central/Eastern Europe and Western Europe. Identify underserved pain categories, buying triggers, and positioning white space that Effectime can own.

---

## Market Segmentation Framework

### Primary Segments to Research
1. **SMB owner-managed companies (20–80 employees)** — minimal HR function, owner involved in ops decisions, pain: time wasted on coordination chaos
2. **Growing companies with a small HR team (50–150 employees)** — 1–2 HR generalists managing everything manually, pain: no system, constant firefighting
3. **Multi-site operations (retail, optical, healthcare, professional services)** — coverage and scheduling complexity, pain: visibility across locations
4. **Tech-adjacent SMBs using Jira/Agile** — hybrid work, dev + non-dev teams, pain: no unified workforce layer across teams

### Geographic Segments
- Hungary (home market): SMB digitization gap, Hungarian-language tooling required
- Czech/Slovak/Polish markets: adjacent, similar pain profile, EU compliance layer adds urgency
- English-speaking international: later stage, benchmark pricing and messaging against global players

---

## Research Methodology

### Step 1 — Competitor Landscape Scan
For each competitor (Personio, Calamari, Factorial, BambooHR, Sage HR, Kenjo, HRWorks, Sympa, Zoho People):
- What market segment do they dominate?
- What language/geography focus?
- What pricing tier?
- What messaging angle do they lead with?
- What features are table stakes vs. differentiators?

Search sources: G2, Capterra, GetApp reviews; competitor websites; LinkedIn company pages; job postings (reveals internal priorities).

### Step 2 — Search Intent Analysis
Research the following intent clusters:
- **Informational**: "how to manage employee leave requests," "attendance tracking template," "HR software for small business"
- **Problem-aware**: "too many leave requests via WhatsApp," "employee scheduling chaos," "Excel leave tracker not working"
- **Solution-aware**: "Personio alternative," "BambooHR too expensive," "HR software Central Europe"
- **Decision stage**: "Effectime review," "workforce management software comparison," "leave management software demo"

Tools: Google Search Console (if available), Google Keyword Planner, Ahrefs/Semrush (if licensed), AnswerThePublic for question clusters.

### Step 3 — ICP Signal Discovery
Look for behavioral and demographic signals that predict buying:
- Company size milestone signals: hiring first HR person, expanding to second office, hitting 50+ employees
- Technology signals: still using Google Sheets / Outlook for leave tracking, recently adopted Jira
- Event signals: year-end absence reviews, compliance audit preparation, failed audit
- Growth signals: job postings for HR manager roles, LinkedIn company growth > 20%

## Pain Category Taxonomy (Seed List — Expand with Research)

| Pain Category | Surface Symptom | Root Cause |
|---|---|---|
| Leave chaos | Leave requests via chat, missed approvals | No system of record |
| Attendance gaps | Tardiness undocumented, disputes at month-end | No trackable check-in/out |
| Coverage failures | Unexpected understaffing, no backup plan | No coverage rule layer |
| Reporting burden | Manual monthly reports, error-prone | No automated HR reporting |
| Multi-site blindness | Manager at site A doesn't know status at site B | No unified view |
| Agile/HR disconnect | Sprint planning ignores who is on leave | No Jira integration with HR data |

---

## Required Outputs

### Output 1 — Market Map
- Segment size estimates (employees × companies, rough addressable count by country)
- Maturity of each segment (digitized vs. greenfield)
- Key buying triggers per segment

### Output 2 — Pain Taxonomy
- 10–15 documented pain categories with severity rating (high/medium/low)
- For each: who feels it most (persona), how often, and what workaround they use today

### Output 3 — Opportunity Spaces
- Positioning white space: what is NO competitor clearly owning?
- Feature gaps: what do buyers request that existing tools don't deliver?
- Geography gaps: where is tooling available but localization absent?
- Tone/messaging gaps: is anyone speaking to operations managers, not just HR?

---

## Research Quality Rules
- Use data from the past 18 months only; the market is moving quickly
- Triangulate every major claim across at least 2 sources
- Flag assumptions that need validation vs. findings that are confirmed
- Output in structured markdown with source citations
- Avoid: vendor-published market size figures without independent corroboration
