# ICP and Buyer Persona Builder

## Purpose
Use this file to define, refine, and operationalize Effectime's Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) and buyer personas. Every piece of content, campaign, and sales motion should be traceable back to one of the personas defined here. Update this document when new customer data challenges the assumptions.

---

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Firmographic Criteria
- **Company size**: 30–150 employees (sweet spot: 50–100)
- **Structure**: Owner-managed or a founder + small management team; HR function is 0–2 people
- **Operational maturity**: Manual or semi-digital; using Excel, Google Sheets, WhatsApp, email for coordination
- **Growth stage**: Stable but growing — recent hiring push, or about to cross a headcount threshold that makes manual processes unsustainable
- **Sites**: 1–5 locations; multi-site companies feel the pain acutely
- **Tech stack signals**: Using Jira or planning to; may already have payroll software but no integrated attendance/leave layer
- **Geography**: Hungary (primary), Czech Republic, Slovakia, Poland (near-term); broader European SMB (future)

### Behavioral / Trigger Criteria
- Recent HR or ops hire (system selection likely)
- Failed an internal audit or had a compliance issue related to attendance or leave
- Experienced a coverage failure that caused a customer or service problem
- Owner directly involved in approving leave or resolving scheduling conflicts — wants to remove themselves from that loop
- Recently hit 50, 75, or 100 employees (critical digitization thresholds)

### Negative ICP (Do Not Target)
- Companies below 20 employees (too simple, low LTV)
- Companies above 300 employees with established HRIS (already have incumbent, long sales cycle)
- Pure remote/no-location companies with no coverage complexity
- Companies that have recently signed a contract with a direct competitor

## Persona 1 — "The Overwhelmed HR Generalist"

**Name archetype**: Kata, 34, HR Manager at a 70-person multi-site optical retail chain

**Job title variants**: HR Manager, HR Generalist, HR Business Partner, People Operations Manager

**Daily reality**:
- Receives leave requests via WhatsApp, email, and in-person — no single source of truth
- Manually updates an Excel tracker for every team; sends it to managers each Monday
- Spends 2–3 hours weekly reconciling attendance data before payroll
- Has no visibility into whether a site will be adequately covered before approving leave
- Gets blamed when something falls through the cracks, even though the process is broken

**Trigger events for buying**:
- Payroll error caused by attendance discrepancy
- Manager complained about being understaffed after leave was approved
- New labor compliance requirement she can't track in Excel
- Second office opened; her current process doesn't scale

**Objections**:
- "I barely have time to implement a new system"
- "Last time we tried software, nobody used it"
- "My manager needs to approve the budget"

**Success metrics**:
- Hours saved per week on admin
- Zero missed leave approvals or attendance disputes at month-end
- Being seen as organized and in control by management

**Preferred channels**: LinkedIn, peer recommendation, HR community groups, webinars

## Persona 2 — "The Operations Manager Who Needs Visibility"

**Name archetype**: Péter, 42, Operations Director at a 120-person logistics/services company

**Job title variants**: Operations Manager, Operations Director, COO, General Manager, Site Manager

**Daily reality**:
- Manages multiple teams or sites and never has a clear view of who is available when
- Relies on team leaders to "just tell him" when there's a coverage problem — finds out too late
- Sprint planning or project allocation is constantly disrupted by untracked leave
- Doesn't think of himself as an "HR software buyer" — he thinks of himself as someone who needs a system that works
- Frustrated by last-minute calls to cover absent staff

**Trigger events for buying**:
- A service failure or customer complaint caused by understaffing
- Discovering that two key team leads were both on leave the same week — approved by different managers
- Trying to plan a project and realizing he has no idea who will be available

**Objections**:
- "I don't have time for another software tool"
- "This sounds like an HR problem, not mine"
- "I need something that my team will actually use"

**Success metrics**:
- No coverage surprises; can see capacity weeks in advance
- Resource planning integrated into Jira sprint work
- Less time spent resolving coordination issues

**Preferred channels**: LinkedIn, peer recommendation, direct outreach, founder relationships

## Persona 3 — "The Founder / Owner Who Wants Control Without Micromanaging"

**Name archetype**: András, 49, founder-owner of a 55-person professional services firm

**Job title variants**: CEO, Managing Director, Founder, Owner

**Daily reality**:
- Still personally approving leave or at least aware of most requests — doesn't want to be, but is afraid of losing control
- No formal HR layer; office manager handles admin, which has become unsustainable
- Gut-feels that something is slipping but can't identify what
- Makes hiring and planning decisions based on incomplete data about team capacity
- Wants systems that "just work" without a long IT project

**Trigger events for buying**:
- Someone key left and revealed how fragile the process was
- Board or investor asked about HR systems in a due diligence context
- Plans to open a new office and realizes the current approach won't scale
- Year-end: realized they have no accurate attendance record for a full year

**Objections**:
- "We're not big enough for this yet"
- "How long does implementation take?"
- "I don't want my team to feel like they're being surveilled"

**Success metrics**:
- Peace of mind: knows the system is handling coordination without his involvement
- Clean data for payroll and compliance without manual work
- Visible ROI: fewer errors, less admin time for him and his team

**Preferred channels**: Direct LinkedIn (founder peer network), referral, word of mouth, event/speaking

### Customer

- TL;DR: A buyer persona is a research-based profile of your ideal customer, encompassing their goals, challenges, and purchasing behaviors. These personas help you personalize marketing, align sales and marketing teams, and improve ROI.
- How to create a buyer persona: Gather data from real customers, identify patterns, and document motivations and pain points.
- Individual customer profiles (they represent groups).
- Customers who are too advanced for your product or service.
- Potential customers who are just too expensive to acquire.
- Actual quotes from interviews: Real words from real customers.
- Survey customers to confirm accuracy.
- Why did you buy this now, as opposed to earlier or later? This will help you understand the critical moments in a customer journey that catalyze a purchase, known as the “buying moment.”.

### Buyer

- How many different buyers and influencers need to be addressed through marketing and sales outreach to generate a typical sale?.
- How do buyers and influencers describe their business or technical problems? What specific words and phrases do they search for when seeking solutions?.
- How do buyers educate themselves on offerings that might meet business or technical needs?.
- Which vendors are considered leaders by buyers and influencers? Which vendors are perceived as being behind?.

- What emerging trends (business or technology) are influencing buyer’s decisions?.

### General Principles

- Demographics: Age, location, income, education, job title.
- Psychographics: Values, interests, lifestyle choices.
- Behavioral patterns: Preferred channels, content consumption habits, buying triggers.
- Goals and challenges: Professional objectives and pain points.
- Static documents that never change.
- People who only engage with your content to gain knowledge.
- Price-focused shoppers with no brand loyalty and high return rates.
