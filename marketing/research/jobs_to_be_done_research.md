# Jobs to Be Done (JTBD) Research Framework

## Purpose
Use this file to apply the Jobs to Be Done framework to Effectime's buyer research. JTBD reveals the real motivations behind purchasing decisions — not "what features do you want" but "what are you trying to accomplish in your situation, and why do existing solutions fail you?" Use this to write sharper messaging, design better demos, and build content that resonates at the decision level.

---

## JTBD Core Principle
Buyers don't buy software. They hire software to do a job they can't get done with their current tools. The job has three layers:
- **Functional**: The practical task to accomplish
- **Social**: How the buyer wants to be perceived by others
- **Emotional**: How the buyer wants to feel (or stop feeling)

---

## JTBD Map by Persona

### Persona 1 — HR Generalist / HR Manager

**Functional jobs**:
- "When I receive a leave request via WhatsApp, I want to log it in a system so I stop losing requests in chat threads."
- "When I need to run the monthly attendance report, I want to pull it in minutes so I don't have to manually reconcile spreadsheets before payroll."
- "When a new employee joins, I want to onboard their leave entitlements quickly so I don't have to update five different files."
- "When I approve leave, I want to see if it creates a coverage problem before I confirm so I don't cause an understaffing incident."

**Social jobs**:
- "I want to be seen as organized and reliable by my manager and the business owners."
- "I want to stop being the person who is blamed when leave tracking breaks down."
- "I want to present clean, accurate HR data when asked — not have to say 'let me check my spreadsheet.'"

**Emotional jobs**:
- "I want to feel in control of my own workload, not constantly reactive."
- "I want to stop dreading Monday mornings when messages have piled up over the weekend."
- "I want to feel like I'm doing professional-grade HR work, not improvising with consumer tools."

---

### Persona 2 — Operations Manager

**Functional jobs**:
- "When I'm planning next month's project resources, I want to see who is available and who is on leave so I stop planning around incorrect assumptions."
- "When a site manager tells me they're understaffed, I want to have already known this a week before so I can respond, not react."
- "When the team is using Jira for sprint planning, I want leave visibility directly in that context so I stop having two separate conversations."

**Social jobs**:
- "I want to be seen as a leader who is in control of operations, not someone who is always surprised by people issues."
- "I want to demonstrate to the owner/CEO that the business runs predictably under my oversight."

**Emotional jobs**:
- "I want to stop getting late-Friday calls about understaffing on Monday."
- "I want to feel like my planning decisions are based on real data, not incomplete information."

---

### Persona 3 — Founder / Owner

**Functional jobs**:
- "When I need to know if we're compliant with labor regulations, I want a reliable record so I'm not caught off guard during an audit."
- "When I'm deciding whether to open a new office, I want to understand current capacity so I can make an informed decision."
- "When an employee asks me directly about their leave balance, I want to be able to answer or point them somewhere rather than checking with HR."

**Social jobs**:
- "I want to appear organized and professional to investors, auditors, and potential hires."
- "I want to stop being the bottleneck for decisions that shouldn't require my involvement."

**Emotional jobs**:
- "I want peace of mind that the business is running without my constant intervention."
- "I want to feel like I've built systems, not a business that only works when I'm watching."

## JTBD Statement Templates
Use these templates to generate new JTBD statements from customer interviews:

> "When [situation / trigger], I want to [outcome / job], so I can [benefit / motivation]."

> "When [situation], I struggle to [job], because [current solution fails to] — and that makes me feel [emotional consequence]."

---

## Discovery Interview Questions

Use these questions in customer or prospect interviews to surface JTBD:

1. Walk me through the last time you dealt with a leave request or attendance issue — what happened, step by step?
2. What did you do before you had any software for this? What was the moment you realized that wasn't working?
3. When things go wrong in workforce coordination, who finds out first — and how?
4. What would have to be true for you to feel like this problem was fully solved?
5. What does your manager/owner say when they notice coordination problems? What do they expect from you?
6. Describe the last time you had to explain a workforce/HR problem to someone more senior. How did that feel?
7. If you could wave a wand and have one thing work automatically, what would it be?

---

## Survey Questions (Quantitative Validation)

For email or in-app surveys targeting HR/ops roles at SMBs:

1. How many hours per week do you estimate you spend on manual workforce coordination tasks? (0–1 / 1–3 / 3–6 / 6+)
2. What is your current primary tool for leave management? (WhatsApp/Telegram / Email / Spreadsheet / Dedicated software / Other)
3. How often do you have a coverage problem that wasn't anticipated? (Weekly / Monthly / Rarely / Never)
4. What is the biggest single frustration in how your team manages attendance and leave? [Open text]
5. What would "solved" look like for you? [Open text]

## Converting JTBD Findings into Messaging

| JTBD Finding | Messaging Application |
|---|---|
| "Stop losing requests in chat" | "All leave requests in one place — not scattered across WhatsApp and email" |
| "Know coverage before approving" | "See coverage impact before you approve, not after" |
| "Clean data for payroll without manual work" | "Attendance data that flows to payroll — no manual reconciliation" |
| "Stop being surprised by understaffing" | "Coverage visibility weeks ahead, not the morning it happens" |
| "Feel like I'm doing professional HR work" | "The system that makes small HR teams look like a bigger operation" |

---

## JTBD Research Cadence
- Run 5–8 customer interviews per quarter during product-market-fit stage
- Synthesize into updated JTBD map after each round
- Compare new JTBD findings against existing messaging — flag drift
- Share raw quotes with content team; real customer language always outperforms copywriter language
