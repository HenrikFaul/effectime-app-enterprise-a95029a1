# Brand Messaging Framework

## Purpose
This is the master messaging architecture for Effectime. Every piece of external communication — from a LinkedIn post to a sales proposal — should be grounded in this document. When new copy is created, validate it against the brand promise, message pillars, and vocabulary rules here.

---

## Brand Promise
**Effectime gives growing companies the operational visibility and coordination structure they need to stop firefighting and start running predictably.**

This is not a tagline. It is the internal compass. Every message should deliver some portion of this promise.

---

## Tagline Options

1. **"Run your workforce. Not the other way around."** — Assertive, outcome-led, clear contrast
2. **"Finally, a clear picture of who's doing what."** — Visibility angle, relatable to ops managers
3. **"Less coordination chaos. More work done."** — Pain-to-outcome, direct
4. **"Workforce coordination for companies that are actually growing."** — ICP-targeted, implies incumbents don't fit
5. **"The system behind teams that run smoothly."** — Credibility framing, positions as infrastructure not tool

**Recommended primary**: Option 1. Use Option 3 for performance ads. Use Option 5 for enterprise/partner contexts.

## Elevator Pitch Variants

### 1-Sentence
Effectime is the workforce coordination system that growing companies use to replace manual leave tracking, scattered attendance data, and coverage guesswork with a single clear view of who's working, who's absent, and whether the team is covered.

### 3-Sentence
Most companies between 30 and 150 employees are running workforce coordination on WhatsApp, spreadsheets, and hope. That works until it doesn't — and when it breaks, it's always at the worst time. Effectime replaces that patchwork with a system that gives HR managers and operations leads visibility and control without a six-month implementation project.

### 1-Paragraph
Running a growing company means managing leave approvals, attendance records, shift coverage, and resource planning across an increasingly complex team — and most companies at this size are still doing it manually. Effectime is the operational system that brings this under control: leave requests that flow through one place instead of scattered channels, attendance that reconciles itself, coverage plans that flag problems before they happen, and Jira-integrated resource visibility for teams running projects alongside people. For HR generalists, operations managers, and founders who want clarity without micromanaging, Effectime turns coordination chaos into a predictable, documented process.

## Tone of Voice Guide

### Core Tone Attributes
- **Direct**: Say what you mean in the fewest words. No throat-clearing.
- **Experienced**: Write from the perspective of someone who has seen this problem many times and knows how to solve it. Not preachy or academic.
- **Practical**: Ground every claim in a concrete operational scenario. Avoid abstract benefits.
- **Confident without hype**: Make claims that can be substantiated. No superlatives without evidence.
- **Human but not casual**: Professional enough for a B2B context; warm enough that a 40-person company feels spoken to, not talked down to.

### Tone in Practice
- Long sentences occasionally work; write paragraphs that breathe.
- Use "you" and "your team" — not "organizations" or "enterprises."
- Use active voice. "Effectime tracks attendance" not "attendance is tracked."
- Questions are effective when they mirror what the reader is thinking.
- Avoid: exclamation points except sparingly; phrases like "at the end of the day" or "in today's world."

## Vocabulary — Approved and Banned Terms

### APPROVED (use these consistently)
- Workforce coordination / workforce management
- Operational visibility
- Leave management / leave tracking
- Attendance tracking
- Coverage planning / coverage rules
- Resource planning / capacity planning
- HR workflows
- Coordination system / operational system
- Growing companies / growing teams
- Multi-site management
- Manual process → automated record

### BANNED / AVOID
- "Revolutionize" — overused, unbelievable
- "Game-changer" — lazy, never use
- "Seamless" — means nothing, often used to hide complexity
- "Powerful" — not differentiated; every tool is "powerful"
- "All-in-one HR platform" — positions against Personio as a category follower
- "Enterprise-grade" — unless specifically addressing a large-company feature
- "AI-powered" — unless a specific AI feature exists and is being described accurately
- "Empower" — HR cliché, avoid entirely
- "Streamline" — overused; replace with the specific outcome
- "Solution" as a standalone noun — "our solution helps..." is weak; name what it does

## Core Message Pillars

### Pillar 1 — Operational Visibility
"Know what's happening across your team before it becomes a problem. One place to see who's on leave, who's available, and whether the coverage is adequate."
*Use for*: ops manager persona, homepage, coverage feature page, LinkedIn content

### Pillar 2 — Replace the Patchwork
"Most growing companies run workforce coordination on three tools that don't talk to each other. Effectime replaces the spreadsheet, the WhatsApp thread, and the calendar invite with one connected system."
*Use for*: HR manager persona, comparison pages, content about Excel and manual processes

### Pillar 3 — Fast to Start, Grows With You
"Live in 2–3 weeks. Start with leave management. Add coverage planning, attendance, and Jira integration when you're ready. No IT project required."
*Use for*: objection handling (implementation fear), onboarding page, founder persona

### Pillar 4 — For the People Who Make the Business Run
"Effectime isn't built for the CHRO of a 5,000-person company. It's built for the HR manager, ops lead, or founder who needs to make a 50–150 person team run predictably without a full-time coordination staff."
*Use for*: ICP targeting, LinkedIn, persona-specific landing pages, ad creative

### Pillar 5 — Proof, Not Promises
"Real records. Real compliance. Real capacity data. When the audit comes, when payroll needs to close, when the investor asks — the data is there."
*Use for*: compliance-sensitive verticals, founder/owner persona, sales proposals
