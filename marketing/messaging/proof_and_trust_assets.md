# Proof and Trust Assets Framework

## Purpose
Proof is what separates a claim from a fact. This file defines the types of proof Effectime needs, how to collect and format each type, and where each type belongs in the marketing and sales funnel. A product without proof loses to a product with proof even when the features are equivalent. Build this asset library continuously.

---

## Proof Type 1 — Customer Quotes and Testimonials

**What it is**: Direct quotes from real customers addressing specific outcomes or objections.

**What makes it useful**:
- Specific and situational (names the problem, not just the solution)
- Mentions a measurable change or tangible improvement
- Comes from a recognizable job title or industry type
- Ideally includes the company name or at least industry + size

**How to collect**:
- Post-onboarding check-in call: "What's different for you now versus 30 days ago?"
- NPS follow-up for high scorers: "What would you tell someone considering Effectime?"
- Annual review interview with power users
- Ask specifically: "Can you describe one thing that used to take much longer that's now just handled?"

**Format by channel**:
- Website: Pull quote card with name, title, company (or industry + size if anonymous)
- LinkedIn: Screenshot-style card with one sentence + attribution
- Sales proposal: Inline quote adjacent to the relevant value claim
- Case study: Full paragraph as narrative anchor

**Where in funnel**: Middle and bottom — addresses consideration and objection stages most effectively.

## Proof Type 2 — Quantified Outcomes

**What it is**: Specific numbers that demonstrate time saved, errors reduced, or costs avoided.

**Examples**:
- "Reduced monthly attendance reconciliation from 3 hours to 15 minutes"
- "Zero missed leave approvals in 6 months after switching from email tracking"
- "Coverage incidents dropped from monthly to zero in the first quarter of use"

**How to collect**:
- Ask customers to estimate time spent before Effectime in customer interviews
- Instrument in-app: response time for leave approvals, report generation frequency
- Survey: "Before Effectime, how many hours per week did you spend on manual coordination?"

**Format by channel**:
- Website: Bold stat cards ("3 hours → 15 minutes") in social proof sections
- Ads: Single-number creative — large number, context, CTA
- Sales deck: Opening slide metric or closing ROI slide
- Email: Opening line of nurture sequence

**Where in funnel**: Top and bottom — grabs attention at awareness, closes at decision.

## Proof Type 3 — Before / After Scenarios

**What it is**: A narrative that walks through how a process worked before Effectime vs. how it works now. Not feature-focused — process-focused.

**Example structure**:
> **Before**: Leave request comes via WhatsApp. HR writes it in a personal notebook, then updates the Excel tracker that evening. On Monday, the manager discovers the team is understaffed. HR spends 45 minutes tracing back who approved what.
>
> **After**: Leave request submitted in Effectime. Coverage check runs automatically. Manager approves with one tap. Calendar updates. Payroll data auto-updates at month-end. No calls, no spreadsheet, no surprises.

**How to collect**: Walk through with customers in interview format — "take me through what this process looked like step by step before you had Effectime."

**Format by channel**:
- Website: Feature section illustration with two-column layout
- LinkedIn: Long post format — "here's what managing leave at a 70-person company looked like before and after"
- Sales deck: Opening "day in the life" slide
- Video: Narrated screen recording showing before workflow vs. Effectime workflow

**Where in funnel**: Top and middle — problem awareness and solution education.

## Proof Type 4 — Integration Trust

**What it is**: Evidence that Effectime connects reliably with tools the buyer already uses.

**Formats**:
- Jira integration: "Works with your existing Jira setup — no data migration" + screenshot of Jira panel with leave data
- Excel compatibility: "Export any report to Excel with one click — share with your accountant as-is"
- Payroll export: "Attendance data exports in standard formats for the most common payroll systems"

**How to present**: Integration logos on homepage trust section; specific integration page per major tool; sales proof ("we already work with Jira — show me your current board and I'll show you how it looks in context").

**Where in funnel**: Middle and late stage — validates technical fit before commitment.

---

## Proof Type 5 — Compliance and Regulatory Trust

**What it is**: Assurance that Effectime produces records that meet local labor law and audit requirements.

**Formats**:
- "Attendance records are timestamped, tamper-evident, and exportable for labor authority review"
- "Configurable leave policies to match Hungarian / Czech / Slovak labor law requirements"
- "Used by companies that have passed labor compliance audits using Effectime records"

**How to collect**: Document any customer who has been audited and used Effectime data successfully. Note any compliance feature that a customer specifically requested and benefited from.

**Where in funnel**: Late stage decision — critical for founder/owner persona who is risk-averse.

---

## Proof Type 6 — Onboarding and Implementation Simplicity

**What it is**: Evidence that setup is fast, not a project.

**Formats**:
- Timeline: "Week 1: org setup. Week 2: team onboarding. Week 3: first full leave cycle live."
- Customer quote: "We were fully live in under 3 weeks. No IT involvement."
- Counter-claim to competitor complexity: Setup is self-serve; no professional services required.

**How to collect**: Track average time-to-first-leave-approval and time-to-full-team-active per customer. Share these as metrics.

**Where in funnel**: Late stage — addresses the implementation objection at the moment of decision.

## Proof Asset Build Priority

| Priority | Asset | Status Target |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 3 customer quotes (one per persona) | Before first paid campaign |
| 2 | 1 before/after scenario (HR persona) | Before first paid campaign |
| 3 | 1 quantified outcome (time saved) | Before first paid campaign |
| 4 | Jira integration screenshot with annotation | Before tech-audience targeting |
| 5 | Compliance statement + 1 supporting customer story | Q2 priority |
| 6 | Full case study (narrative + metrics) | Q2-Q3 priority |

---

## Proof Asset Usage Rules
- Never use a quote without explicit permission from the customer
- Always attribute to the most specific title available (not "a user" — "HR Manager, 80-person optical retail chain")
- Quantified claims must be sourced from a real customer and documented internally
- Do not invent or extrapolate metrics — if you don't have the data, use a scenario-based estimate and label it clearly
