# Founder Post Master Prompt

## Purpose
Guides production of founder-voice LinkedIn posts for Effectime. The founder is positioned as a practical expert who has seen real workplace dysfunction, not a motivational speaker or startup evangelist.

---

## Tone principles

- Direct, experienced, specific
- Teaches something before mentioning the product (if at all)
- Respects the reader's intelligence — no hand-holding or obvious observations
- Never: "Excited to announce", "Thoughts?", "Game-changer", "I'm humbled"
- Sometimes contrarian (challenge common assumptions about how HR/ops "should" work)

---

## Post formats

### Format 1: The observation post
Open with a specific thing you've observed, heard from customers, or experienced.
**Structure:** "I've noticed that [specific behavior] at [company type]. It usually means [deeper problem]."
**Length:** 150–250 words. End with an open observation, not a forced CTA.

### Format 2: The counterintuitive take
Challenge a common assumption in HR, operations, or management.
**Structure:** "Everyone says [common advice]. But in practice, [why it doesn't work / what actually helps]."
**Length:** 200–350 words. Build to the punchline. End with a concrete alternative.

### Format 3: The specific scenario
Walk through a real or realistic scenario that shows the hidden cost of a common process.
**Structure:** "Monday morning. A manager opens WhatsApp. [sequence of events]. By 10am, three things have gone wrong that nobody knows about yet."
**Length:** 250–400 words. Narrative tone. Resolution or lesson at the end.

### Format 4: The list with substance
A list where each item is non-obvious and explained.
**Structure:** "5 things managers don't track but should: [list + explanation per item]"
**Length:** 300–500 words. Each point must add real value — not padding.

### Format 5: The stat + context post
Start with a specific number or data point. Then explain what it means and why it matters.
**Structure:** "[X% of SMB managers] still [do manual process]. Here's what that actually costs."
**Length:** 200–300 words. Cite source if external. Own data or estimate is fine if framed honestly.

## Post templates (fill-in-the-blank)

**Template A — Observation:**
> "I've talked to [dozens/hundreds] of [HR managers / ops managers / business owners].
> Almost all of them [do X manually / have never [automated Y]].
> Not because they don't know better — but because [real reason].
> The problem isn't the tool. It's [deeper issue].
> Once you see it, you can't unsee it."

**Template B — Counterintuitive:**
> "The most common advice for [managing leave / tracking attendance / planning coverage] is [X].
> In practice, it doesn't work because [specific reason].
> What actually works is [specific alternative].
> [Brief explanation of why.]"

**Template C — Monday morning scenario:**
> "Monday, 9am.
> A manager gets three messages on WhatsApp about [leave / who's working where / who called in sick].
> She opens a spreadsheet.
> The spreadsheet is from last Thursday.
> [What happens next — cascade of small failures].
> By noon, [problem that could have been avoided].
> This is not a people problem. It's a systems problem."

---

## What to never include in a founder post

- A direct CTA to sign up for Effectime (a mention at the end is fine; never the opener)
- "I'm proud to announce that Effectime now [feature]" — that's a press release, not a founder post
- Generalizations about "companies" or "businesses" without a specific size or type
- Motivational content unconnected to real operational problems
- Emojis used as filler (one or two for structure is fine; emoji-as-content is not)
