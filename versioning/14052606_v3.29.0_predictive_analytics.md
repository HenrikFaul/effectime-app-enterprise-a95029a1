## What the strategy doc asks for

> "Forecasting labor costs 90 days out, predicting turnover risk based on
> scheduling patterns, identifying which teams will be under-resourced
> during peak periods before it becomes a crisis."

## What v3.29.0 ships

### 1. Labor cost forecast (next N months)

`analytics_labor_cost_forecast(workspace_id, months_ahead)`:
- Per-month projection: sum over active members of
  `salary_band_config[business_role] || 3000 EUR/month`
- Adjusted by approved-leave fraction (member on long leave for the
  whole month → contributes 50% of their base cost; partial leave →
  linearly scaled between 50% and 100%).
- Configurable per workspace via `enterprise_workspaces.salary_band_config jsonb`.

### 2. Absence risk per member

`analytics_absence_risk_scores(workspace_id)`:
- Per-member 0-100 score from:
  - 180-day approved leave count × 8
  - 180-day sick-leave count × 12 (extra weight for sick)
  - +15 if recent rate exceeds the year-long pace (acceleration signal)
- Returns sorted descending so the highest-risk members are first.
- Rule-based by design — per the strategy doc's privacy-first stance,
  no PII is sent to external ML services.

### 3. Coverage risk heatmap (next 90 days)

`analytics_coverage_risk_heatmap(workspace_id, days_ahead)`:
- Per-day count of members on approved + pending leave.
- Color: green (<15% out), yellow (15-29%), red (≥30%).
- Returns ISO DOW for grid rendering.

## UI

- Top absence-risk members (badges color-coded by score range).
