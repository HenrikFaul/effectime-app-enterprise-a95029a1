## Problem and evidence

The byte-recovered v3.22 clock and v3.30 marketplace migrations relied on
default/public ACL behavior. A fresh read-only linked inventory proved that
`anon`, `authenticated` and `service_role` effectively held every table
privilege on all five recovered tables, including PostgreSQL 17 `MAINTAIN`, and
all three roles could execute all seven routines. Every routine used
`search_path=public`; the QR SECURITY DEFINER body called `gen_random_bytes`
without a schema qualifier. The linked server is PostgreSQL 17.6, the target
tables/routines and pgcrypto trust chain are owned by `postgres`, the exact ten
RLS policies are present, and the seven canonical LF-normalized routine bodies
match the recovered source. The linked queries were read-only.

## Implemented boundary

- Adds one forward-only PostgreSQL 17+ migration; historical recovered SQL is
  unchanged.
- Preflights ordinary table kind/owner/RLS, exact browser-role isolation, the
  exact ten permissive policy names/commands/roles/predicates, routine
  signature/owner/language/volatility/return/config/source, QR defaults and
  execution metadata, plus pgcrypto schema/extension/function ownership and
  extension membership. Any drift fails before mutation.
- Removes PUBLIC/anonymous access and browser writes/maintenance; authenticated
  keeps table reads and exactly six product RPCs. The internal Haversine helper
  is not browser-executable.
- Preserves the exact effective `service_role` table, column and routine
  privilege state around every revoke, including inherited PUBLIC rights.
- Forward-replaces only `clock_generate_qr`, preserving OID/signature/owner/ACL/
  defaults/metadata and business behavior while binding entropy to
  `extensions.gen_random_bytes(integer)` and setting a `pg_catalog`-only path.
  The other six bodies remain immutable and receive only the path hardening.
- Adds postconditions for effective table/column/routine ACLs, source hashes,
  QR metadata and exact routine config; the migration is idempotent and repairs
  supported ACL/path drift without touching data or policies.

## Compatibility, rollout and rollback

The migration requires PostgreSQL 17+ because `MAINTAIN` is part of the exact
privilege contract. Apply only after migration-history reconciliation and a
restored-production-like inventory proves the same owners, policy predicates,
routine hashes/config and role graph. Rollout is database first, schema-cache
reload, negative anon/auth ACL smoke, positive tenant RPC smoke, then the shared
web/Android/iOS client candidate. There is no separate mobile data plane.

Do not roll back by broadly granting defaults. If operational rollback is
required, first archive the pre/post catalog inventory and restore only the
reviewed privilege/config delta; preserve the schema-qualified QR binding. A
normal source revert is safe before database apply.
